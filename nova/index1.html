Strawberry

Fruit

Strawberries are bright red, juicy fruits known for their sweet flavor and nutritional benefits, including high vitamin C and antioxidant content. They are versatile in culinary uses, from fresh consumption to desserts and beverages. Thriving in temperate climates, strawberries are a popular choice for both their taste and health advantages.

Hervesting

Gently pull the ripe berries from the plant, ensuring they are fully red and have a firm texture. Strawberries grow best in sandy loam soil with a pH of 5.5 to 6.5.

Disorder

Strawberries can be affected by disorders such as gray mold (Botrytis cinerea), powdery mildew, and various fungal leaf spots.

Characteristics

Strawberries (*Fragaria* genus) are herbaceous perennials with compound leaves, stolons for asexual reproduction, a fibrous root system.

Nutritional Benefits

Strawberries are rich in vitamin C, manganese, antioxidants, and dietary fiber while being low in calories and free of fat and cholesterol.
