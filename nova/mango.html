Mango

Fruit

Mango is a tropical fruit known for its sweet, juicy flesh and distinctively fragrant aroma, popular worldwide for its refreshing taste and versatility in culinary uses.

Hervesting

Mango trees thrive in well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils with a pH range of 5.5 to 7.5. They prefer warm climates and are typically harvested during dry seasons, ensuring optimal fruit quality and yield.

Disorder

Mangoes are susceptible to diseases like anthracnose, powdery mildew, and bacterial black spot, which can affect fruit quality and yield.

Characteristics

The scientific characteristics of mango (*Mangifera indica*) include being a tropical evergreen tree with alternate, oblong leaves, panicles of small, fragrant flowers, and a drupe fruit with a single large seed.

Nutritional Benefits

Mangoes are rich in vitamin C, vitamin A, fiber, and antioxidants like beta-carotene and polyphenols, contributing to immune support, eye health, digestion, and overall well-being.
