Cantaloupe Seeds

Seed

Cantaloupe seeds are small, oval-shaped, and encased in a fibrous, netted outer layer. They are edible and rich in nutrients, often used for planting or snacking.

Hervesting

Cantaloupe seeds are typically sown directly into warm, well-drained soil once the danger of frost has passed. They prefer soil with a pH between 6.0 and 7.0 and are best planted in spring or early summer for a fruitful harvest later in the season..

Disorder

Cantaloupe seeds can be affected by diseases such as powdery mildew, fusarium wilt, and anthracnose, which can impact plant health and fruit production.

Characteristics

The scientific characteristics of cantaloupe seeds include being part of the *Cucumis melo* species, with a typical oval shape and a hard outer shell that protects the embryonic plant inside.

Nutritional Benefits

Cantaloupe seeds are rich in protein, healthy fats, and fiber, providing essential nutrients such as iron, magnesium, and potassium.
